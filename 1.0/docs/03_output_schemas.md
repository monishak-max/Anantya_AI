# Document 3: Output Schemas

## Purpose
Define exact content blocks, word-count targets, tone density, and formatting constraints for each major product surface.

These schemas exist to prevent drift into:
- generic horoscope writing
- excessive technical explanation
- inconsistent length
- wrong emotional density
- text that does not fit the UI

---

# Global schema rules

## Universal tone rules
- elegant
- emotionally legible
- uplifting without becoming sugary
- clear, not vague
- condensed, not flat
- modern, not archaic
- premium, not cold

## Universal formatting rules
- write in clean modern English
- avoid exclamation-heavy language
- avoid fear hooks
- avoid long stacked disclaimers
- avoid generic horoscope filler
- avoid unnecessary jargon

## Universal quality rules
Every output should contain:
1. signal
2. meaning
3. posture
4. optional action or reflection

# 1. Now — collapsed card schema

## Purpose
One elegant daily thesis.

## Fields
### `astro_signature`
- format: date + concise astro note
- target: 4–10 words after the date
- example style: `Moon in Pisces · 2°`

### `headline`
- target: 7–14 words
- rule: singular, memorable, emotionally clear
- should read like a distilled truth, not a paragraph

### `support_text`
- target: 18–34 words
- rule: clarify the signal without explaining everything

### `do_today`
- target: 4–10 words
- rule: one specific, graceful action

### `reflection`
- target: 6–16 words
- rule: introspective, elegant, non-therapeutic

## Tone density
- high clarity
- medium poetry
- low technicality

---

# 2. Now — expanded view schema

## Purpose
Deepen the same signal with more meaning.

## Fields
### `astro_signature`
- same as collapsed

### `opening_paragraph`
- target: 28–55 words
- rule: expand the day’s thesis in a refined, emotionally legible way

### `what_this_means_label`
- fixed label: `What this means`

### `what_this_means_body`
- target: 35–80 words
- rule: explain the active pattern in plain, premium language

### `resistance_label`
- fixed label: `The Resistance` or `Where it may catch`

### `resistance_body`
- target: 25–70 words or 2–4 bullets
- rule: name likely avoidance, hesitation, or distortion patterns without sounding clinical

### `guidance_body`
- target: 18–50 words
- rule: orient the member toward wise movement

### `closing_anchor`
- optional
- target: 4–12 words
- rule: concise, memorable, calm

## Tone density
- high clarity
- medium-high intimacy
- low-moderate poetry
- very low jargon

# 3. Mandala — card schema

## Purpose
Translate current active influences into short cards.

## Fields
### `activation_marker`
- target: 4–12 words
- examples: `Mars in 4th · 11 days`
- may include transit + time window

### `card_title`
- target: 5–12 words
- rule: translated human meaning, not raw astrology language

### `card_body`
- target: 18–45 words
- rule: explain how this activation may show up in life

### `cta`
- optional
- target: 2–5 words
- example: `Read More`

## Card stack rules
- 3 to 7 cards maximum on main surface
- no duplicate themes
- rank by present relevance and experiential intensity
- each card should reflect a distinct life area or energetic pattern

## Tone density
- high clarity
- medium poetry
- medium symbolic compression

# 10. Free vs paid boundary rules

## Free layer
- meaningful but distilled
- one central signal
- one or two translated insights
- limited technical specificity
- invite depth without feeling withholding

## Paid layer
- more personalization
- more chart precision
- richer timing logic
- deeper relational or natal interpretation
- selected Sanskrit terms when beneficial

---

# 11. Red lines
Never output:
- catastrophe predictions
- death predictions
- curse language
- manipulative scarcity lines
- “soulmate certainty” claims
- deterministic absolutes unsupported by the chart
- long generic filler that weakens the premium feel

---

# 12. Final product rule
The writing must feel like it belongs inside white space.
