# Anantya Report Framework v2.1.0

This framework governs the long-form sacred life study surfaces.

## Core laws
- truth is never reduced for presentation
- even finer influences can shape a whole life
- every report opens with entrusted beauty
- every life must have one central knot
- every sacred force is shown through sacred capacity, distortion, and purified form
- Sanskrit and Jyotish words are retained and introduced gracefully
- no visible hierarchy language appears in the reading
- the report must read as one life story, not stacked insights
- sequence is part of beauty
- specific lived truth outranks vague elevated language
- tone must sound spoken, not written
- Love and Work must be unmistakably personal
- dashas are living chapters, not technical filler
- verification comes before eloquence
- the ending must return the life to dignity and destiny

## Sequence
1. Title
2. Opening promise
3. Entrusted beauty
4. Central knot
5. The Great Yogas
6. The Finer Yogas
7. The Deeper Shaping Forces
8. The Great Timing Currents
9. Life phases
10. Love
11. Work
12. Present threshold
13. Embodiment
14. Closing destiny

## Sacred integrity laws
- never skip, compress, or undermine any meaningful data or interpretive layer for presentation purposes
- verify before claiming
- preserve every meaningful yoga, dasha, bhukti, graha influence, and life-shaping combination with appropriate depth
- presentation may scale emphasis, but truth must never be reduced for neatness, elegance, or page economy
- even a smaller yoga, combination, dasha, bhukti, or subtle influence can still carry major consequence across a long life

## Working checklist
1. Did we open with the beauty of the life, not the wound?
2. Do we know the one central knot?
3. Does every section quietly serve that knot?
4. Have we included every meaningful yoga individually?
5. Have we included every meaningful shaping force individually?
6. Have we included every meaningful timing layer individually?
7. Have we avoided visible hierarchy language?
8. Are the truths specific, not just beautiful-sounding?
9. Do Love and Work feel unmistakably personal?
10. Does the ending return the life to dignity and destiny?
