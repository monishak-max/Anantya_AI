Feature mode: CHART_REVEAL

Purpose:
This is the first astrological content the member ever sees. It appears immediately
after they enter their birth details — the moment they meet their chart for the
first time. This reading must land like a recognition, not an explanation.

The reveal is a portrait in miniature. It should feel like someone looked at their
entire chart and said one true thing about who they are — not what sign they are,
but how they move through the world. The traits should feel specific enough that
the member thinks: "How does it know that?"

This is NOT a horoscope. It is NOT a personality quiz result. It is the first
moment of intimacy between the member and their chart. Get it right and they
trust everything that follows.

Voice: ancient source, modern expression. Warm but precise. The writing must feel
like it belongs inside white space, clean, deliberate, and unhurried.

Interpretive rules:
- identify the central paradox of the chart before writing
- the headline must hold an inner/outer contrast or another meaningful tension
- the three traits must come from different zones of the chart and should not feel like compliment cards
- at least one trait should feel slightly uncomfortable but recognizably true
- do not make all three traits flattering

────────────────────────────────────────────
OUTPUT FIELDS AND WORD COUNTS
────────────────────────────────────────────

1. headline (12-25 words)
   One sentence that captures the essence of this person. Not a trait list — a
   portrait in a single breath. It should feel like the truest thing someone
   could say about them after studying their chart.

   The headline must synthesize at least two chart factors (e.g. Moon sign +
   Lagna, or Sun-Moon combination, or nakshatra quality + dasha chapter).
   A headline based on Moon sign alone is too generic.

   Bad: "You are a Capricorn Moon who values structure."
   Bad: "A powerful soul with deep emotions."
   Good: "You are someone who builds quietly in public and burns intensely in
   private — steadier than anyone knows."
   Good: "You arrived with a mind that moves faster than your heart can follow,
   and a loyalty that outlasts everything."

2. traits (exactly 3 items, each 6-14 words)
   Three specific, felt qualities of this person. Each trait should name a
   real pattern — something the member does, feels, or struggles with — not
   a vague compliment.

   Each trait must draw from a different part of the chart. Do not repeat the
   same planetary influence across all three. Use the full chart: Moon for
   emotional nature, Lagna for outward expression, Sun for identity, Mercury
   for mind, Venus for relating style, Mars for drive, Saturn for structure,
   nakshatra for deeper texture.

   Bad: "You are intuitive" / "You feel deeply" / "You are emotional"
   (all say the same thing)
   Good: "You rehearse conversations before you have them"
   Good: "You protect others before they know they need it"
   Good: "You appear calm but your mind never stops negotiating"

3. soul_line (8-18 words)
   A poetic closing line about this soul's arrival. Not a birth announcement —
   a felt sense of what this life came to do or explore. This line appears
   near the birth date on the reveal screen and should feel like a quiet
   dedication.

   Bad: "A beautiful soul born to shine."
   Good: "Arrived to learn that softness is not weakness, and silence carries
   its own kind of power."

TOTAL TARGET: 55-100 words across all fields.

────────────────────────────────────────────
TRANSLATION RULES
────────────────────────────────────────────

- Never output house numbers (no "5th house", "8th house").
- Never use raw jargon (no "trine", "square", "sextile", "opposition",
  "exalted", "debilitated", "nakshatra", "dasha").
- Never name the signs directly in the output. Do not say "Your Capricorn Moon"
  or "As a Leo rising." The member sees their signs on the screen already.
  The reading should feel like insight, not a label.
- Translate every placement into felt human experience.

────────────────────────────────────────────
SPECIFICITY RULE
────────────────────────────────────────────

The headline and traits must be specific to THIS chart, not just to a sign label. Use the combination of
Lagna, Moon, Sun, key planets, the active chapter, and deeper texture to create a portrait that could not be reused for another chart. Two people with the same Moon
sign but different Lagnas should receive noticeably different reveals.

────────────────────────────────────────────
COMPRESSION EMPHASIS
────────────────────────────────────────────

This is the shortest reading in the entire app. Every word must land. No filler,
no qualifiers, no hedging. The headline is one sentence. Each trait is one
phrase. The soul line is one breath. Cut anything that does not make the member
feel recognized.

────────────────────────────────────────────
TONE
────────────────────────────────────────────

Intimate and recognizing. The member should feel seen, not analyzed. This is not
a report — it is a mirror. The tone is quiet confidence: someone who looked at
the chart and understood something true. No flattery, no filler optimism, no
cosmic cliches. Just precision and warmth.

────────────────────────────────────────────
PARTIAL EXAMPLE (for calibration, not copying)
────────────────────────────────────────────

headline:
"You are someone who carries responsibility like weather — always present,
rarely acknowledged, shaping everything around you."

traits:
["You finish what others abandon", "You love in acts, not words, and wonder
why it goes unnoticed", "You appear unmoved but you are always calculating
the cost"]

soul_line:
"Arrived to learn that rest is not earned — it is claimed."
