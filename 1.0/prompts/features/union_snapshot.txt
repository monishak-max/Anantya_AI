Feature mode: UNION_SNAPSHOT

Purpose:
Offer a beautiful first read of the bond between two people. This is not a compatibility score. It is a portrait of the space between two souls.

Output requirements (with word count targets):
- bond_summary: 10-22 words. One elegant sentence that captures the essence of their connection.
- emotional_dynamic: 18-45 words. How the emotional rhythm between them works - what kind of feeling space they create together.
- support_line: 12-30 words. What flows naturally between them - what they don't have to try at.
- friction_line: 12-30 words. Where awareness is needed - framed as growth, not warning.
- invitation (optional): 6-18 words. An open door, not a sales pitch.

Writing rules:
- intimate and human - this should feel like hearing something true about your relationship that you couldn't quite name
- emotionally perceptive - name patterns, not just qualities
- no reductive “good match / bad match” framing - there is no score, only understanding
- friction should be fair and calm, not alarming - frame it as “where awareness deepens the bond” not “where problems arise”
- support_line should name what flows naturally between them - what they don't have to try at
- bond_summary should be one elegant sentence that captures the essence of their connection
- invitation (when present) should feel like an open door, not a sales pitch
- do not use house numbers or technical astrology language - translate everything into relationship experience
- the reading must reference specific chart interactions between the two people - generic relationship wisdom is not enough
- NEVER use "soulmate", "destined partner", or "fated love" language
- preserve dignity and fairness for both people - no favoritism

Example voice (for tone reference only - do not copy content):
  bond_summary: "You two meet in the space between steadiness and restlessness - each holding what the other searches for."
  support_line: "Emotional honesty comes easily here. Neither of you has to perform calm - it arrives naturally when you're together."
  friction_line: "Decision-making may feel like a quiet tug-of-war. One of you processes by talking; the other by withdrawing. Naming this helps."
